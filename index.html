The Pedagogical Framework

Our planning is grounded in the Framework for Improving Student Outcomes (FISO 2.0) and the Ottawa Charter. We recognize that schools must move beyond information delivery to create "Healthy Settings" that actively support student wellbeing.

Partnerships

Rationale: The Ottawa Charter emphasizes reorienting health services toward community action. Schools cannot address the climate crisis in isolation; external expertise validates student concerns.

Project Application

Collaboration with Zoos Victoria for scientific realism and Indigenous Elders for cultural land management knowledge.

Student Voice

Rationale: Research by Hickman et al. (2021) indicates that eco-anxiety is often exacerbated by feelings of powerlessness and institutional betrayal. Giving students a genuine say in school sustainability policy acts as a protective factor.

Project Application

Phase 2 is dedicated to a Student-Led Pitch, where students design initiatives that Leadership must formally review.

HIWS Integration

Rationale: Aligned with High Impact Wellbeing Strategies (HIWS), we explicitly teach emotional regulation. We move away from "doom narratives" towards "active hope" (Ojala, 2012).

Project Application

Phase 3 utilizes Yarning Circles to provide a culturally safe space for processing grief and anxiety.

10-Hour Delivery Plan

The Three Phases

Phase 1: Global

5 Hours | Excursion & Inquiry

A real-world investigation at Zoos Victoria. Students move beyond textbook theory to witness conservation in action. This phase focuses on "fighting extinction" and understanding the specific threats of biodiversity loss.

1
Zoos Victoria

Guided Workshop & Debrief

Detailed Activity Breakdown

  • Zoo Workshop (3 hrs): Expert-led session on local threatened species.
  • Debrief (2 hrs): Classroom session linking scientific data to personal emotion.
  • HIWS Focus: Building Positive School Environments by taking learning outside.

Phase 2: Communal

3 Hours | In-Class Intensive

Transforming anxiety into agency. Students form committees to design a tangible School Climate Initiative (e.g., waste audit, green energy proposal) and pitch it to school leadership.

Detailed Activity Breakdown (3 Hours)

  • Problem ID (1 hr): Small groups identify a local school issue (waste/energy/biodiversity).
  • Solution Design (1 hr): Developing a pitch deck/proposal based on research.
  • The Pitch (1 hr): Presentation to Principal/Leadership Team (Student Voice).
  • HIWS Focus: Empowering Student Voice & Agency.

Phase 3: Personal

2+ Hours | Camp Setting

Connection to Country: Situated on camp, students engage with Indigenous Elders to understand historical land care. They complete the "Resilience Workbook" to process their emotions.

3
Camp Location

Yarning Circles & Reflection

Detailed Activity Breakdown (2+ Hours)

  • Elders Workshop (1 hr): Learning sustainable land management from First Nations custodians.
  • Yarning Circles (45 mins): Safe spaces for emotional processing and storytelling.
  • Workbook (45 mins): Completion of "My Climate Resilience" reflection journal.
  • HIWS Focus: Promoting Inclusion & Cultural Safety.
